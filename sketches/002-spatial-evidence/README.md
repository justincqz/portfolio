# Spatial Evidence

Homepage concept 002 for Justin Chong's portfolio. Open `index.html` directly in a browser; there are no dependencies, assets, fonts, or build steps.

## Design stance

This direction makes model space the organizing idea rather than treating technical work as a set of interchangeable cards. A warm editorial canvas carries the narrative, while an embedded dark representation field gives Metalynx Scatter an immediate, project-specific interaction. The overall effect is intended to feel precise and formidable without adopting a generic dark developer aesthetic.

The page prioritizes the hiring story in this order: builder and technical leader, Metalynx end-to-end ownership, Kikan systems thinking, Aival leadership in regulated delivery, then supporting engineering depth and active experiments.

## Key choices

- The hero point field is inline SVG, not canvas. Its 48 authored samples, three class clusters, overlap region, centroids, outliers, axes, and labels render as a meaningful static representation before JavaScript runs.
- Progressive enhancement adds class, confidence, and cohort recoloring; pointer inspection; touch selection; and spatial arrow-key navigation with Enter/Space cohort selection. Metadata is mirrored through a live text readout.
- Metalynx, Kikan, and Aival use deliberately different evidence forms: an interactive representation and analysis pipeline, a staged execution map, and restrained leadership facts.
- Claims and language follow the supplied content strategy. Metalynx testing is explicitly attributed to individual researchers and explicitly disclaims organizational adoption or endorsement. Kikan is identified as early-stage dogfooding rather than proven production autonomy. Aival stays at approved high-level scope.
- The résumé is the primary action in both the opening and close. Selected work is the secondary hero action, while professional links remain subordinate.
- System fonts, a warm off-white base, near-black ink, one site accent, and data-only visualization colors maintain the light-led editorial direction without decorative gradients.
- Accessibility includes semantic landmarks, heading order, a skip link, visible focus, 44px controls, adjacent diagram descriptions, live interaction feedback, a no-script explanation, and reduced-motion handling.
- Mobile layouts recompose rather than only shrink: the point field stays contained at full width, controls become a full-width row, Kikan becomes a two-column sequence, and evidence sections collapse into readable editorial lists.

## Trade-offs

- The SVG field is authored rather than generated so it works as content without JavaScript and stays deterministic. This creates more markup than canvas but improves fallback quality, accessibility, and inspectability.
- The point field is a portfolio-specific explanation of the Metalynx workflow, not a recreation of its historical OpenGL interface. The caption makes that distinction explicit.
- Internal routes express the intended information architecture. Final implementation should connect the contact, LinkedIn, and Scholar routes to verified destinations.
- On small screens, the plot preserves the complete field rather than using a larger transformed crop that can create horizontal overflow. Expanded pointer targets and keyboard navigation keep every sample reachable, while the adjacent explanation carries the fine-grained detail.

## Best use

Use this direction when memorability and AI-product specificity matter most, particularly for Staff/Principal, Technical Lead, and AI-product engineering audiences. It is strongest when the eventual case studies can continue the spatial navigation language and provide verified product media. The precision-editorial direction may be preferable when maximum recruiter scan speed is more important than experimental character.
