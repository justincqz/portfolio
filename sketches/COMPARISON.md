# Homepage Sketch Comparison

These concepts share the same core story and evidence, but they optimize for different reading behaviors. The comparison is intentionally decisive about those differences without naming a final winner.

| Criterion | 001 / Precision Editorial | 002 / Spatial Evidence | 003 / Process Atlas |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hierarchy | **Strongest conventional hierarchy.** The hero, proof strip, three unequal project treatments, engineering depth, lab, and operating approach form the clearest top-to-bottom hiring narrative. | **Strong but deliberately split.** The interactive model field competes productively with the hero copy; memorable, though less immediately linear than 001. | **Architecture-led hierarchy.** Kikan receives the dominant visual and narrative position, with Metalynx and Aival clearly secondary. This is focused rather than balanced. |
| Scanability | **Fastest recruiter scan.** Large editorial headings, numbered sections, compact proof, and repeated rules make the page easy to skim without using the interactions. Its density is controlled, not light. | **Good after the hero, slower within it.** The field rewards inspection and the later evidence layouts scan well, but the opening asks for more attention than the other concepts. | **Fast for systems-oriented readers, moderate for generalists.** The proof strip and project summaries are concise, while the eight-stage atlas invites a deeper pause early in the page. |
| Experimental character | **Lowest, by design.** Asymmetry, rust accents, and bespoke project compositions keep it distinctive, but it remains recognizably editorial and hiring-manager friendly. | **Highest product-specific experimentation.** The model-space interaction makes the portfolio feel inseparable from AI analysis work rather than like a styled résumé. | **Highest systems-diagram character.** The connected execution atlas is unusual and technically expressive, but feels more like an architecture publication than a product showcase. |
| Technical depth | **Broadest distribution of depth.** Product, orchestration, regulated delivery, performance work, and operating principles all receive meaningful space. | **Deepest interaction-level demonstration.** The authored sample data, multiple color modes, spatial keyboard navigation, and analysis pipeline show technical care directly. | **Deepest process exposition.** Kikan's stages, gates, outputs, failure path, and evidence boundary make systems thinking especially legible. Metalynx is intentionally less interactive here. |
| Mobile risk | **Low to moderate.** Most layouts collapse cleanly, controls remain large, and full-bleed media is bounded. The long page and information density are the main costs. | **Highest of the three.** The dense full-width SVG field, five-link mobile navigation, and two-column Kikan sequence require the most device testing, even though explicit mobile rules are present. | **Moderate.** Replacing the atlas map with an ordered control list is robust, and the navigation simplifies aggressively. The risk is mainly page length and the tall stacked architecture inspector. |
| Best fit | Hiring processes where **rapid comprehension and balanced builder-leader evidence** matter most: Technical Lead, Staff/Principal, platform, data, and AI-product roles. | Audiences where **AI-product specificity, interaction craft, and memorability** should lead, especially when richer Metalynx case-study media can follow. | Roles where **systems thinking, autonomous engineering workflows, and technical process design** are the primary signal, including Staff/Principal and architecture-heavy leadership work. |

## Decision Framing

- Choose **001** when the first question is, “Can a hiring manager understand the full case quickly?”
- Choose **002** when the first question is, “Will the portfolio demonstrate a distinctive technical-product point of view?”
- Choose **003** when the first question is, “Will the reader understand how complex engineering work is structured and controlled?”

Each direction keeps the résumé as the primary action, selected work as the secondary hero path, and claims bounded by the available evidence. The decision should therefore turn on audience and emphasis, not baseline completeness.
