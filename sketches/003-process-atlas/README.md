# Process Atlas — Homepage Concept 003

## Design stance

Process Atlas turns Kikan's execution stages into the homepage's organizing visual system. The direction is dense, precise, and technical, but remains editorial: hierarchy, captions, project writing, and evidence lead; the connected atlas is not presented as a monitoring dashboard or product interface.

The warm paper canvas, near-black ink, compact annotations, and one high-visibility acid accent create a controlled technical publication. There are no external assets, decorative gradients, faux terminals, logo clouds, or interchangeable project cards.

## Key choices

- Kikan is the architecture hero. Its eight approved workflow stages form a selectable atlas, with arrow-key navigation and a live evidence panel.
- The Kikan evidence boundary is explicit: current evidence is early-stage self-dogfooding, not proven production autonomy or a reliability, success-rate, or time-saving claim.
- Metalynx Scatter is treated as spatial product evidence with a newly drawn conceptual PCA/point-cloud view. It is clearly labeled as not being the historical product UI.
- Aival uses a restrained, deliberately high-level operating model. It communicates leadership, regulated delivery, and deployment range without exposing proprietary architecture or client details.
- Immediate proof, compact engineering stories, operating principles, AI Lab context, and a strong closing action make the page useful beyond the project showcase.
- `View résumé` is the primary action in the navigation, hero, and close. `Explore selected work` is the secondary hero action.
- Semantic landmarks, a skip link, visible focus, 44px minimum interactive targets, reduced-motion handling, and adjacent diagram descriptions are built in.
- Mobile replaces the desktop atlas map with a full-width ordered control sequence rather than shrinking the diagram into illegibility.

## Trade-offs

- The architecture visual is conceptual and CSS/SVG-based. It communicates sequence and system boundaries without claiming to reproduce Kikan's internal implementation.
- Direct email and professional-profile links should be added after their destinations are verified; this concept currently routes the close to the site's contact page.
- The concept links to the intended site routes, but this standalone sketch does not implement those destination pages.
- The Metalynx visualization is intentionally static and representative. A production case study could progressively enhance it with a real, sanitized dataset after source inspection.

## Best use

This direction is strongest when the primary hiring signal should be systems thinking: Staff/Principal engineering, technical leadership, platform/data systems, and AI-product engineering roles. It is especially effective for readers who want a fast positioning statement followed by inspectable technical process and defensible evidence.

Open `index.html` directly in a browser. It has no dependencies, build step, external images, or external fonts.
